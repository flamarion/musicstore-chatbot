# Deployed Engineer - Technical Task - LangChain and LangSmith Demo

**Goal:**

Experience what it is like to demo a combination of our open-source software (OSS) and LangSmith (our commercial product) to a prospective customer.

**Context:**

We often work with customers getting started with the LangChain ecosystem and need to produce compelling demos. Treat this demo as if you are demoing to a prospective customer that wants to start building with our OSS. Your audience will include a mixture of business and technical stakeholders. The customer has done some initial agentic development work but has been unsuccessful in delivering a reliable agent in production. The customer does not know much about the differences in our OSS and how LangSmith fits into the LangChain stack. The prospective customer also doesn’t understand why LangSmith is important.

**Exercise:**

For this exercise, we will simulate working with a music store trying to build a customer support bot. We will use the Chinook dataset, a sample music store database that contains information about customers, employees, customer orders, and the music offered at the store.

Your job is to create a compelling demo of a customer support bot via LangChain’s OSS and LangSmith. Part of the goal is to try to make this as realistic as possible by choosing and extending OSS capabilities that would be most valuable for a real music store and production agent.

**Requirements:**

- Explain our OSS (LangChain, LangGraph and Deep Agents) and how LangSmith fits in.
- Use LangChain to orchestrate the support bot. The support chatbot should be capable of at least two different “areas” of work (ex. music recommendations and working with customer transactions)
  - We recommend using the LangChain agent abstractions `create_agent()` [](https://docs.langchain.com/oss/python/langchain/agents)or `create_deep_agent()`
- Connect to the Chinook dataset - <https://raw.githubusercontent.com/lerocha/chinook-database/master/ChinookDatabase/DataSources/Chinook_Sqlite.sql>
- Run your app in LangSmith Studio (ask for help if you’re struggling setting this up!)
- Add OSS features to improve the agent (hint: Middleware)
- Demo features of LangSmith including any differentiating features.

**Things to think about:**

- What should the bot be able to do, and why?
- What does the cognitive architecture look like (LangSmith Studio might help with the visualization) and how does it work? Why did you chose this architecture?
- How do you ensure the customer can only see info about themselves?
- Why is LangSmith important when building agents? What features are important to show and in what order?

**Tips for success:**

- Be comfortably familiar with LangChain’s OSS.
- Be prepared to answer customer questions related to your agent’s architecture and the design decisions involved.
- Showcase capabilities related to robust, reliable agent engineering.
- Do not go for breadth of tools. Pick a short list (2-4) interesting business problems that the chatbot should be able to solve.
- Don’t overthink the data. This is not an exercise on your SQL skills.  Look at the data and weave a narrative around it. Don’t get bogged down in the details here as the dataset is not perfectly fit to the problem.
- No need to spend time building a custom UI. Instead, just use LangSmith Studio to show things like chat interactions.
- A couple slides are okay if you find that is an effective way to tell the story, but don’t spend much time building or presenting slides.
- Use the slack channel to ask questions along the way! We know we aren’t providing any starter code, but we encourage questions and will provide guidance in slack!
- We added a link to chat.langchain.com at the bottom. This is a great place to find information around our OSS!
- Consider using a coding agent to assist. You can connect to our docs via MCP - <https://docs.langchain.com/use-these-docs>.
- Be prepared to show and explain your code (even if generated by a coding agent).
- Most importantly - tell the business a compelling story that shows them how they can build something great with LangChain!

**Timeline:**

- Devise a game plan: We recommend writing a short plan of attack in the slack channel before you start coding to make sure you’re tackling the problem in a good way. Ask clarifying questions to make sure you’re on track. (15 - 30 mins)
- Build the customer support bot. (~2-3 days max)
- Demo app to the team (45 mins total - 35 minutes of demo + 10 minutes for customer questions throughout and at the end). Spend no more than 10 minutes explaining business value and the company, then jump into the demo. Make sure to cover:
  - LangChain as a company, our OSS and how LangSmith fits in (no more than 10 minutes here)
  - LangSmith Demo
    - A realistic flow of questions and interactions the bot is equipped to handle
    - The underlying cognitive architecture + tools
    - Important features of LangSmith and how they tie into each other and the agent engineering process (do not show deployments)
  - Friction log, what was harder than expected

# What to Expect Throughout the Rest of the Interview Process

---

## Peer Technical Interview

This is a technical conversation with a Deployed Engineer focused on understanding your technical strengths, depth, and problem-solving approach.

Topics may include:

- Software engineering
- Infrastructure/platform concepts
- Agentic systems
- Data/ML workflows
- Debugging and troubleshooting approaches

We do not expect candidates to be equally deep across all areas. The goal is more to understand:

- where you are strongest technically,
- how you think through problems,
- and how you communicate technical ideas under pressure.

---

## Take-Home Demo Exercise

The take-home is designed to simulate a realistic customer-facing DE workflow.

You’ll build and present a demo showcasing:

- LangChain OSS capabilities
- LangSmith functionality
- Agent architecture/design decisions
- A compelling customer narrative around why these tools matter

The exercise is intentionally somewhat open-ended. Strong submissions typically:

- Make thoughtful architectural choices
- Focus on a few meaningful workflows rather than trying to demo everything
- Clearly explain tradeoffs and implementation decisions
- Connect technical functionality back to customer/business value
- Demonstrate curiosity, learning agility, and technical storytelling ability

The final presentation includes both the live demo itself and discussion/Q&A around your architecture, technical choices, and overall approach.

You are strongly encouraged to use the Slack channel throughout the exercise to:

- Ask clarifying questions
- Share assumptions or proposed approaches
- Surface friction points
- Engage collaboratively with the team as you work through the assignment

The team is not evaluating perfection. They are evaluating how you think, communicate, learn, and navigate ambiguity while building something practical and compelling.
